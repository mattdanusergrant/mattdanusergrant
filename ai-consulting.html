Decision Tournament

Decision-making

A five-judge panel for any decision. Take (or generate) the options, score each one from five tailored viewpoints, then merge the winners into a single, defensible pick.

What it does

Most “what should I pick?” answers hide their tradeoffs. This runs your options past five distinct personas — an enthusiast, a skeptic, an expert, a pragmatist, a rival — scores every contender on a 1–10 matrix, and then assembles the best possible answer from across the field instead of just crowning one. Great for names, copy, designs, product calls, and life decisions.

Case Study Forge

Portfolio · Anonymizer

Turn a completed interview design test into a polished, fully anonymized HTML case study — company, product, names, and confidential numbers stripped out; only your thinking kept.

What it does

Take-homes and design challenges are some of your best work, but they’re usually under NDA and can’t go in a portfolio as-is. This reads your finished response, builds an anonymization ledger (real → stand-in for every company, IP, and figure), and renders a clean, self-contained HTML case study that shows how you think without exposing who it was for. Works for any discipline that runs take-homes.
